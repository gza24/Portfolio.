Online Portfolio: Projects

Lucas Garza


The Scratch Project was the first project that was assigned to the class to start off the year. For this assignment me and my partner decided to make a platformer game, or try to atleast. The point of the game was to collect keys throughout the levels and escape a volcano. The water spirit, the character you controlled, was trapped inside with a wizard that could let him out. As the levels increased the difficulty also increased. There was lava and if you touched it you would respawn at the top left corner.


My role for the project was the art and the coding, while my partner did the documentation. I decided to create the water sprite using a bright light blue color and then creating it as if a squid became somewhat human. I also gave it a nose. Then I got pictures off the internet that could fit our levels and I reshaped them to fit the canvas but I also made sure that they weren't too blurry. When I was coding the respawn part, where if you touched the lava you respawned back at the top, I noticed that I didn't give a warning that it would happen, so the user would have to figure it out on their own. And when I was coding the controls for the sprite, I made a mistake. I mixed up the look controls on accident. So when you pressed the left arrow it would go left but it wold face towards the right. This project had a lot and I mean a lot of errors on it.


After this project was finished I learned the basics of how to program on scratch, and how to persevere after every error that I caught. I gained some responsibility in this project which will help me in the long run.
